Coconut Prawn Curry

  • Time: 20 minutes
  • Serves: 4
Ingredients .....Quantity
Curry Paste ....1-2(tsp)
Onion 1 whole onion
Green Chilli One slice
Coconut Cream One tin
Raw peeled prawns 500g
Chopped tomatoes One tin

Step 1: Fry curry in oil, add onion, green chilli,chopped tomatoes and cocnut cream. Simmer until the onion is soft

Step 2:Add prawns and cook until the prawns are cooked through.Stir in a handful of chopped coriander and a squeezed lime

Soso Ya Mwamba

  • Time: 45 minutes
  • Serves: 6
Ingredients .....Quantity
Whole raw chicken One
Onion 2 whole onions
Red Chilli Three
Peanut butter One jar
Chicken stock cubes Three
Chopped tomatoes One tin
Nutmeg Three

Step 1: Season the chicken using 2 stock cubes, salt and 1 grounded nutmeg. Leave to season for 15 minutes

Step 2: In a pan, fry the onions until brown and then add the chopped tomatoes.

Step 3: In a separate pan, fry the seasoned chicken until cooked

Step 4: In a large pot move the fried tomatoes and onions, add water and the fried chicken. Add 1 stock cube 1 nutmeg before adding in the peanut butter. Let it simmer for 25 minutes. Add salt and pepper for taste and three red chillies. Serve with white rice.

水煮魚

  • Time: 25 minutes
  • Serves: 2
Ingredients .....Quantity
Boneless fish fillet 250g
Salt One pinch
White pepper One pinch
Cornstarch One tsp
Cooking oil 1/2tsp
Dried chilli 15
Sichuan Peppercorn Two tsp
Cloves of garlic Two
Scallions Two
Sichuan Chilli bean paste Two tsp
Hot water 400ml

Step 1: Slice the fish fillet diagonally. Marinate with salt, white pepper, rice wine & corn starch.

Step 2: In a wok (or a deep frying pan), fry dried chilli and Sichuan pepper in oil over a low heat until fragrant (do not burn them). Chop coarsely when cooled. Set aside.

Step 3: In the same wok, heat up oil over a medium heat, stir in celery. Cook for 1.5 minutes or so (30 seconds if using bean sprouts instead). Transfer to a serving bowl. Set aside.

Step 4:Heat up oil in the wok, fry garlic, ginger and spring onion. Add Sichuan chilli bean paste and chilli powder. Pour in water (or chicken stock). Bring it to a full boil.

Step 5: Gently place the fish slices into the wok. When cooked, pour the fish and the soup onto the vegetable.

Step 6: Top with fried spices and coriander. Heat up oil then pour over to sizzle the spices. Serve immediately with plain rice.
