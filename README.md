        <h2 id="Prawn-curry">COCONUT PRAWN CURRY</h2> <br> <br> <br>
        
        <p> Makes 4 Servings</p>
        <p> Curry Paste</p>
        <p> Onion</p>
        <p> Green Chillies</p>
        <p> Coconut Cream</p>
        <p> Raw Peeled Prawns</p>
        <p> Vegetable Stock Cube</p></div>
        <br>
        
        
      <div class="curry-steps">
        <h2><strong>Step 1:</strong></h2> <p>Fry 2tsp. of curry paste  in oil, chop one whole onion and add it in along with 2  green chillies 1 tin of chopped tomatoes and 1 tin of coconut cream. Simmer until the onion are soft.</p>
        
        <br>
 <h2><strong>Step 2:</strong></h2> <p>Add 500g of raw peeled prawns and cook until the prawns are cooked through.Stir in a handful of chopped coriander and a squeezed lime.</p>
        <p><em>Source:<a href="https://www.olivemagazine.com/recipes/fish-and-seafood/best-prawn-curry-recipes/"> Website </a></em></p></div>

       <div class="soso">
       <h2 id="soso">SOSO YA MWAMBA</h2>
       <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/50/Moambe_chicken.jpg/220px-Moambe_chicken.jpg"alt="Chicken cooked in peanut soup with rice"width="380">
      
      <br>
    
        <p> Whole Raw Chicken</p>
        <p> Onion</p>
        <p> Red Chillies</p>
        <p> Peanut Butter</p>
        <p> Chicken Stock Cubes</p>
        <p> Chopped Tomatoes</p>
        <p>Nutmeg</p>
        <br>
        <h2><strong>Step 1:</strong></h2> <p>Season the chicken using 2 stock cubes, salt and 1 grounded nutmeg. Leave to season for 15 minutes</p>
        <h2><strong>Step 2:</strong></h2> <p>In a pan, fry the onions until brown and then add the chopped tomatoes. </p>
        <h2><strong>Step 3:</strong></h2> <p>In a separate pan, fry the seasoned chicken until cooked</p>
        <h2><strong>Step 4:</strong></h2> <p>In a large pot move the fried tomatoes and onions, add water and the fried chicken. Add 1 stock cube 1 nutmeg before adding in the peanut butter. Let it simmer for 25 minutes. Add salt and pepper for taste and three red chillies. Serve with white rice.</p>
         <p><em>Source: <a href="https://www.pinterest.fr/pin/383228249524406234/">Website</a></em></p></div>
         
         <div class="yu">
        <h2 id="fish">水煮魚</h2>
        <img src="https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4f/Sliced_Fish_in_Hot_Chili_Oil.jpg" alt="image of Sichuanese dish 水煮魚"width="380">
        
      <br>
      <p> Makes 6 Servings</p>
        <p> Boneless Fish Fillet</p>
        <p> Salt</p>
        <p> White Pepper</p>
        <p> Cornstarch</p>
        <p> Cooking Oil</p>
        <p> Dried Chilli</p>
        <p>Sichuan Peppercorn</p>
        <p>Cloves of garlic</p>
        <p>Scallions</p>
        <p>Sichuan Chilli bean paste</p>
        <p>Hot water</p>
        <br>
        
        <h2><strong>Step 1:</strong></h2> <p>Slice the fish fillet diagonally. Marinate with salt, white pepper, rice wine & corn starch.</p>
        <h2><strong>Step 2:</strong></h2> <p>In a wok (or a deep frying pan), fry dried chilli and Sichuan pepper in oil over a low heat until fragrant (do not burn them). Chop coarsely when cooled. Set aside. </p>
        <h2><strong>Step 3:</strong></h2> <p> In the same wok, heat up oil over a medium heat, stir in celery. Cook for 1.5 minutes or so (30 seconds if using bean sprouts instead). Transfer to a serving bowl. Set aside.</p>
        <h2><strong>Step 4:</strong></h2> <p>Heat up oil in the wok, fry garlic, ginger and spring onion. Add Sichuan chilli bean paste and chilli powder. Pour in water (or chicken stock). Bring it to a full boil.</p>
        <h2><strong>Step 5:</strong></h2> <p> Gently place the fish slices into the wok. When cooked, pour the fish and the soup onto the vegetable.</p>
        <h2><strong>Step 6:</strong></h2> <p>Top with fried spices and coriander. Heat up oil then pour over to sizzle the spices. Serve immediately with plain rice.</p>
         <p><em>Source: <a href="https://redhousespice.com/sichuan-boiled-fish">Website</a></em></p></div>
